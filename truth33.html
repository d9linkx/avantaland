Truth #33

Money won't fix a broken engine; it just makes the crash more spectacular

Executive Summary

I saw two founders take different paths. One raised $2 million and used it to hire a big team, rent a fancy office, and run expensive ad campaigns. The money allowed him to ignore bad metrics and user feedback until the company spectacularly crashed. The other founder bootstrapped. The lack of money forced her to be resourceful, listen intently to users, and find true product-market fit. Funding is not a solution; it's an amplifier. It makes a good business grow faster, but it makes a broken one fail more dramatically. Build resilience first, business second.

The paradox of early funding reveals itself quietly, then all at once

Summary

Funding can only buy you time, but it cannot manufacture resilience. It can increase momentum, but it cannot create product-market fit.

The best founders treat money like oxygen. Necessary, invisible, and never the point of the journey.

So, build your grit first, and build your business second. Treat funding as a tool, not a trophy. The constraints you resent today will quiet shape the strength that will save you tomorrow.
