Truth #27

Feedback is a weapon; use it like a boss, or your competitors will

Executive Summary

I almost lost a major client by defensively arguing with their feedback. With every criticism, I felt my face grow hotter and I explained why they were wrong. They didn't sign. The next time I got similar feedback, I forced myself to shut up and ask, "Tell me more." That conversation led to a game-changing insight. Feedback isn't a personal attack; it's priceless market intelligence. Your ego is the enemy of progress. The founder who welcomes criticism builds with data; the defensive founder builds with guesswork. Stop protecting your ego and start protecting your business.

Actionable Checklist

I almost destroyed my first company during a user feedback session. We were demoing our product to a potential enterprise client. The kind of deal that could make our quarter.

The head of operations, a sharp woman named Madam Rita, started pointing out flaws. "This workflow doesn't match how my team actually works," she said. "And this data visualization is confusing."

With every criticism, I felt my face grow hotter. I started explaining. "Actually, if you look here, the workflow is designed to streamline..." I talked over her concerns, defending my creation. After ten minutes of this, she held up a hand. "I can see you're very committed to your vision," she said politely. "We'll think about it."

They never signed.

The exact same week, we got nearly identical feedback from another prospect. But this time, I forced myself to shut up and listen. "Tell me more about why that doesn't work," I said. What emerged was a fundamental insight about their operational reality that completely changed our approach to the product.

The lesson: Feedback isn't personal criticism. It's market intelligence. Your product is a hypothesis; feedback is the data that proves or disproves it. Once you decide to shut up and listening to feedback, you‚Äôll find the truth, which will set you free.

The fatal instinct of defensive listening

Most founders treat feedback like a personal attack. They armor up, defend their territory, and miss the goldmine of insights being offered. The natural human response is to protect your creation. But in businesses, this instinct is fatal.

Summary

Feedback is the raw material of iteration. The more defensive you are, the less raw material you have to work with. The founder who welcomes criticism builds with market data; the defensive founder builds with guesswork.

Stop protecting your ego and start protecting your runway. Your ability to listen to things you don't want to hear determines how quickly you'll build things people actually want.
