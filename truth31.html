Truth #31

Hiring before revenue is a gamble; hire slow, fire fast

Executive Summary

Ferdinand made his first hire when his bank account had exactly $18,000 left. They hadn't earned a single dollar of revenue, but they had a "promising pipeline" and "strong investor interest." Big words, right?

Why premature hiring is organizational suicide

Hiring before achieving revenue isn't just risky. It's often organizational suicide. You're simply making one of the most expensive, irreversible decisions your business will ever make.

Summary

Early hiring is like taking on a co-pilot when you're still figuring out how to fly the plane. Wait until you're at least airborne and know your destination.

When you do hire, be more rigorous about evaluating fit than you are about evaluating skill.
