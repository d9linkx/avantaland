Truth #13

Talking to users before coding is mandatory

Executive Summary

I have a graveyard of code that never saw the light of day. The most expensive tombstone is "Project Sisyphus", a social analytics platform I spent six months building before speaking to a single user.

Actionable Checklist

I have a graveyard of code that never saw the light of day. The most expensive tombstone is "Project Sisyphus", a social analytics platform I spent six months building before speaking to a single user.

I built a sophisticated algorithm for "meaningful engagement." My first beta tester, Chloe, took one look and said: "I don't care about engagement. Does this post actually drive sales?"

The next four calls were the same. I soon figured out that I had built a solution to a problem nobody had.

The cost: Six months of work, thousands in server fees, and countless late nights.

The reminder: If you're not talking to users, you're just writing fan fiction about your own genius.

Summary

Code is liability; user understanding is an asset. Every line of code represents complexity added and flexibility lost. The best founders don't fall in love with their solutions; they fall in love with their users' problems.

Talk to your customers first. Only after then should you code. Do this every time.
