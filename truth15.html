Actionable Checklist

I once built a product for a problem that was six months away from existing. It was 2018, and I was convinced privacy regulations would create a massive market for "compliance automation."

I spent months building a beautiful platform. I was early, I was visionary, and I was completely, utterly bankrupt within a year.

The problem was simple: my potential customers didn't feel the pain yet. When I'd ask about data compliance, they'd say, "Yeah, that might be important later."

They were worried about next quarter's revenue. They were worried about this week's payroll. They were worried about today's customer complaints.

My brilliant solution was a "someday" problem competing against their "right now" emergencies.

The turning point came during a demo when an e-commerce founder interrupted: "Forget that, can your system tell me why my checkout abandonment rate jumped 15% this week?"

I saw the genuine panic in his eyes. That was a pain he felt in his gut, today. I pivoted to solving that instead. It wasn't visionary or sexy, but the founders didn't say "interesting". They asked "how do I get this right now?" and "how much does it cost?"

The lesson: They were already feeling that pain, and they were actively searching for relief.

Summary

The market rewards painkillers, not vitamins. Don't build something that might be important someday. Build something that solves a bleeding wound today.

Find the thing that makes people say "Ouch!" and then be the bandage. Your customers' present pain will always fund your business more reliably than their future problems.
